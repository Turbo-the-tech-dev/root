## üìä Part 4: Case Studies (Contrast & Compare)

### Case Study 1: Data Breach Response

**Scenario:** User data leaked. 100,000 affected.

| Approach | Action | Outcome |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Ethical** | Immediate disclosure, free credit monitoring, fix root cause | Short-term pain, long-term trust |
| **Unethical** | Hide, deny when discovered, minimal response | Short-term gain, long-term destruction |
| **Gray** | Delayed disclosure (legal window), minimal help | Legal but reputation damaged |

**Analysis:**
- Ethical approach costs more upfront but preserves trust
- Unethical approach saves money but destroys company when discovered
- Gray approach is legal but stakeholders feel betrayed

**Lesson:** Transparency compounds. Secrets decay.

---

### Case Study 2: AI Job Displacement

**Scenario:** AI can replace 50% of customer service team.

| Approach | Action | Outcome |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Ethical** | Announce early, retrain, transition support, natural attrition | Higher cost, loyal workforce, good PR |
| **Unethical** | Replace silently, no support, maximize short-term profit | Lower cost, destroyed morale, bad PR |
| **Gray** | Natural attrition + hiring freeze | Legal, slower, still harms some |

**Analysis:**
- Ethical approach treats humans as ends, not means
- Unethical approach maximizes shareholder value exclusively
- Gray approach is legal but avoids hard decisions

**Lesson:** How you do things matters as much as what you do.

---

### Case Study 3: Security Vulnerability

**Scenario:** You find a critical vulnerability in a popular product.

| Approach | Action | Outcome |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Ethical** | Private disclosure, reasonable timeline, coordinate fix | Users protected, company can fix |
| **Unethical** | Exploit, sell on dark market, or public shame | Profit or fame, users at risk |
| **Gray** | Public disclosure after 90 days (Google model) | Pressure to fix, but users exposed |

**Analysis:**
- Ethical approach balances all stakeholders
- Unethical approach is selfish, potentially criminal
- Gray approach has merit but timeline is debated

**Lesson:** Responsible disclosure protects everyone.

---

### Case Study 4: Pricing During Crisis

**Scenario:** Natural disaster. Demand spikes for essential product.

| Approach | Action | Outcome |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Ethical** | Maintain prices, ration fairly, maybe discount | Lower profit, community trust |
| **Unethical** | Price gouge 10x, maximize profit | High profit, destroyed reputation, legal risk |
| **Gray** | Small increase (supply/demand), within legal limits | Legal, but feels exploitative |

**Analysis:**
- Ethical approach builds lifelong loyalty
- Unethical approach is illegal in many places, always wrong
- Gray approach is legal but stakeholders notice

**Lesson:** Crisis reveals character.

|---------|----------------|
| **Code of Ethics** | Written, accessible, enforced |
